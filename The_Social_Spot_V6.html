📖 Understanding the Algorithm

🎯 Packaging (CTR)

CTR measures how well your topic and thumbnail convert browsing into viewing. If CTR is low, your content is invisible to potential new fans regardless of quality.

⏱️ Satisfaction (AVD)

AVD is the strongest signal of viewer satisfaction. High AVD tells YouTube to keep serving the video because it keeps users on the platform longer.

📈 Subscriber Growth Projection

Based on your virality score and views, estimate expected subscriber conversion rate. Industry average is 0.5–3% of total views converting to subscribers.

What is Sub Conversion Rate?
This is the percentage of total video views that result in a new subscriber. For every 1,000 views, a 1% rate = 10 new subs. This rate is influenced by: niche relevance (viewers staying in your topic), call-to-action strength (asking people to subscribe), content consistency (a clear brand or recurring series), and thumbnail/title alignment (viewers who feel the video matched expectations). New channels typically see 0.5–1.5%, established creators 1.5–3%, and viral outliers up to 5–10%.

❓ FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

A complete guide to every page, feature, and metric in the Virality Checker dashboard.

⚡ Virality Checker

The Virality Checker scores your video's current performance on a 0–100 scale by weighing four key metrics the YouTube algorithm uses to decide distribution:

  • CTR (Click-Through Rate): The percentage of people who clicked your thumbnail after seeing it. Industry benchmark is 3–5%. Below 2% signals a weak thumbnail or title. This accounts for 25 points max.
  • AVD % (Average View Duration): What percentage of your video the average viewer watched. YouTube lowers its targets as videos get longer — a 10-min video at 50% is excellent; a 2-min video needs 80%+. Worth up to 30–45 points depending on video age.
  • Hook Retention (30s): The percentage of viewers who made it past the first 30 seconds. This is the most predictive metric for long-term performance. Under 50% means most viewers left before they had a chance to value your content. Worth up to 15 points.
  • Engagement Rate: (Likes + Comments) ÷ Views. A 1–3% rate is healthy. High engagement tells the algorithm viewers feel strongly about the content. Worth up to 10 points.
  • New Video Toggle: If your video is under 48 hours old, check this box. It shifts the scoring to weight AVD more heavily and reduces Hook scoring, since fresh videos haven't accumulated long-term retention data yet.

Score guide: 0–39 = Underperforming, 40–74 = Strong Growth, 75–100 = Viral Breakout 🚀

🤖 Algorithm Tracker

Shows the five distribution tiers YouTube pushes content through and what performance benchmarks you need to hit at each stage. Enter your video length to get length-adjusted AVD targets for each tier. Use this to diagnose where your video stopped getting pushed — if it stalled at Tier 2, your CTR likely dropped when served to a colder audience.

💰 Monetization Tracker

Tracks your progress toward YouTube's Partner Program (YPP) requirements: 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours in 12 months. Enter your current hours, a start and target date, and your daily watch-hour velocity (from YouTube Studio). The tracker calculates your ETA and shows how adding live stream hours accelerates progress, since stream watch time counts directly toward the 4,000-hour threshold.

  • Daily Watch Hrs from Videos: Found in YouTube Studio → Analytics → Reach → Watch Time
  • Stream Hours / Week × Avg Concurrent Viewers = stream watch hours generated per week
  • Avg View Duration: Used to calculate how many daily video views you need to hit your velocity target
💹 RPM Predictor

Simulates your potential monthly and annual ad revenue based on your view volume and RPM (Revenue Per Mille — what you earn per 1,000 monetized views, after YouTube's 45% cut). Adjust the min/max RPM sliders to match your niche. The benchmark table at the bottom shows real-world RPM ranges by content category. Use the Shorts section separately since Shorts RPM is dramatically lower ($0.03–$0.08) due to the separate Shorts ad revenue pool.

📈 Channel Projections

Projects your subscriber and view trajectory over 6 and 12 months based on your current stats. Enter your current subscribers, monthly views, CTR, average watch time, upload frequency, and sub conversion rate. It generates three growth scenarios:

  • Current Path: Where you end up if nothing changes
  • CTR Optimization: Impact of hitting your target CTR
  • More Uploads: Impact of increasing upload frequency
  • Optimistic Path: Best-case combining better conversion, more views, and higher upload frequency

Sub Conversion Rate is the % of total views that result in a new subscriber. Industry average is 0.5–3%.

⚖️ Channel Compare

Lets you manually enter publicly reported stats for up to 3 YouTube channels to compare them side-by-side. Since the YouTube API doesn't expose CTR or watch time publicly, this tool is intentionally manual — you supply what you can find from published interviews, Social Blade estimates, or creator transparency posts. The tool calculates derived metrics like Views Per Subscriber, Revenue Per Video, and Content Efficiency Score and ranks each channel across every category.

Tip: Social Blade (socialblade.com) provides subscriber counts and estimated monthly views for any public channel.

🎬 Video Tracker

A time-series tracker for monitoring individual video performance over multiple days. Add a new video title, then log daily entries with impressions, views, CTR, AVD%, and likes from YouTube Studio. The tracker detects trends between data points and generates an automated insight (e.g., "Algorithmic Spike" or "Fatigue Alert"). You can also connect your YouTube Data API key to auto-fetch view and like counts — CTR and AVD must still be entered manually as the API does not expose those. Use Export to save your data as a JSON file, and Import to restore it.

📱 Social Analytics

Covers TikTok and Instagram Reels separately with their own scoring systems, since each platform weights different signals:

  • TikTok prioritizes Completion Rate (did viewers finish the video?) and Share Velocity (did they send it to others?). The 3-second hook retention is the gating metric — below 40%, the algorithm stops expanding your batch.
  • Instagram Reels prioritizes Saves (bookmark-worthy signal) and Sends/DMs (brings new users into the app). High replay rates (watch time over 100%) are the strongest Explore page trigger.

Both sections include a distribution score (0–100) and a distribution tier breakdown showing what metrics you need at each stage of algorithmic expansion.

⏰ Publishing Hub

Generates an optimal publishing schedule based on your content format (Long-Form vs. Short-Form), niche, timezone, and upload frequency. Long-form videos should be published 2 hours before the peak audience window to allow YouTube's indexing and notification delivery to complete. Short-form content should be published during the peak window since the first 30–60 minutes determine whether the algorithm expands the batch. The weekly schedule view highlights your best publish days based on niche-specific audience activity patterns.
